# Slide Template Deconstruction Notes

Source: `/Users/iraoliverfernando/Downloads/Slide Learning - Deconstruction.pptx`  
Rendered review artifacts: `tmp/pptx-deconstruction/analysis/contact-sheet-01.png` through `contact-sheet-03.png`  
Slide count: 66, 16:9 widescreen.

## Useful Patterns Observed

### 1. Small Stage, Large Negative Space
Many slides intentionally keep the active content region to 40–70% of the canvas. The rest is not empty by accident; it creates breathing room, makes the slide feel designed, and gives decorative geometry somewhere to live without competing with content.

**Gengar adaptation:** allow active-stage slides where content occupies a deliberate bounded region, especially for title, agenda, quote, milestone, and transition slides. Do not automatically stretch every object to full width.

### 2. Corner Anchors Create Identity
Several templates use oversized abstract shapes clipped at corners or edges: quarter circles, blob/star forms, stepped tiles, and off-canvas geometry. These act as recurring anchors and brand memory devices.

**Gengar adaptation:** use corner/edge anchors as brand punctuation, but keep them out of the reading path. Use them to stabilize the frame, not to decorate every gap.

### 3. Repeated Top Metadata System
The deck often repeats small top-corner metadata: slide number, section name, or minimal navigation mark. This creates continuity without heavy headers.

**Gengar adaptation:** use a light metadata rail for analysis decks: section label, slide number, source/caveat status. Keep it quiet and consistent.

### 4. Mixed Light/Dark Mode Families
The template alternates between light playful slides and dark business slides. The strongest sequences keep mode families together; the weaker moments jump too abruptly.

**Gengar adaptation:** decks may include light and dark modes, but mode transitions need narrative purpose: cover/section break/appendix shift. Do not alternate modes randomly.

### 5. Progressive Disclosure Structures
Agenda, milestone, roadmap, and workflow slides use numbered lanes, timelines, and cards arranged as steps. These are useful when the content itself is procedural.

**Gengar adaptation:** use step/lane layouts only when the story is genuinely sequential. If the content is analytical, use evidence/implication layouts instead.

### 6. Editorial Asymmetry
Several effective slides place a dominant headline or visual on one side and a smaller supporting object on the other. The split is rarely equal; one side clearly wins.

**Gengar adaptation:** prefer dominant/support splits (`2/3`, `3/2`, `5-span`) over equal columns. Equal columns are reserved for comparisons where both sides truly have equal weight.

### 7. Metric Slides Need Spatial Discipline
The template uses open metric typography, simple labels, and generous spacing. The better metric slides avoid enclosing every number in a box.

**Gengar adaptation:** use metrics as open typography with source/caveat nearby. Avoid giant empty KPI cards unless building a dashboard artifact.

### 8. Section Families, Not One Universal Layout
The deck contains multiple template families: milestone celebration, business plan, competitive analysis, investor pitch, workflow, thesis, strategy. Each family has its own rhythm.

**Gengar adaptation:** our slide system should define families by job: cover, agenda, argument, evidence, workflow, comparison, quote, appendix. Do not use one house layout for all slide jobs.

## Failure Modes To Avoid

- Decorative shapes becoming louder than the claim.
- Text blocks floating without a clear grid relationship.
- Mode switching without story purpose.
- Cards used because layout feels empty rather than because comparison demands it.
- Tiny labels becoming unreadable in contact-sheet review.
- Template variety replacing narrative coherence.

## Creative Director Decisions

1. Use deliberate active stages: not all slides need full-canvas content.
2. Adopt quiet metadata rails for section, slide number, source, and caveat status.
3. Treat corner geometry as a reusable brand anchor, not random decoration.
4. Use mode transitions as story punctuation only.
5. Prefer dominant/support compositions over equal columns.
6. Make every slide belong to a job family before choosing layout.
7. Preserve contact-sheet legibility: titles, dominant objects, and slide jobs must read at thumbnail size.

## Reviewer Acceptance Criteria

- The slide has one visible dominant read at thumbnail size.
- The content region is either intentionally full-bleed/full-span or intentionally staged; it is not accidentally cramped.
- Metadata rails are consistent and quiet.
- Decorative geometry does not interrupt the reading path.
- Light/dark mode changes are justified by narrative role.
- Cards, lanes, and timelines are used only when the content structure requires them.
- Each slide job is named in the slide brief before layout begins.
